Attention

We often believe that we read text objectively, but our attention is constantly guided by visual emphasis. Certain words become more important simply because they are highlighted.

In digital reading, highlight is not just decoration. It creates hierarchy and tells the eye where to go first. A marked phrase begins to feel more urgent, even if the meaning of the sentence has not changed.

This page treats highlighting as a reading tool. It asks how visual emphasis shapes memory, interpretation, and focus.
